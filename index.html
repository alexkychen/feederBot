Monitor fish feeding activity through computer vision

Powered by Raspberry Pi

Concept

Fish feeding activity has a direct link to fish health, but it can be difficult to measure in large rearing systems, particularly in absence of fish keepers. Toward this end, I have developed a computerized device that can automatically release fish food and measure the number of fish during a feeding event. Through time-series analyses and machine-learning algorithms, it can determine whether fish feeding activities deviate from normal patterns, and subsequently send alerts to fish keepers when necessary.

Fish feeding model: Fish feeding activity is quantified by counting the number of fish appeared within a feeding zone through time.

Two feeding patterns (red and orange) deviate from the normal pattern (blue). Red line demonstrates slow feeding - fish have decreasing appetite. Orange line indicates that the number of feeding fish has decreased.

Electronic Components

Fish FeederBot is built based on Raspberry Pi - an open source, inexpensive, and expandable single board computer, which connects to stepper motor and relay modules for feeder and pump control.

Vision Sensor

Raspberry Pi Camera is used to capture images, and each image is then processed by computer vision programming to measure the number of fish.

Feeder Module

Feeder is driven by a stepper motor and the quantity of fish food can be precisely manipulated.

Relay Module

To ensure the camera captures images of fish feeding, a relay module is used to temporarily turn off the power of pumps that produce water flow.

3D Printed Housing

Electronic components are protected by 3D printed plastic cases, which can be customized to fit various rearing systems.

Applications

  • Laboratory Research - Fish FeederBot can assist to study how fish feeding activities change under various conditions. Feeding events (e.g., feed quantity and timing) are programmable, so the data can be systematically collected for further analyses. Results stored in databases help researchers analyze the data in an efficient manner.

  • Aquaculture & Aquarium - Through computationally monitoring fish feeding activity, aquaculture farmers or fish keepers can be notified if the fish have decreasing appetite and possibly suffer from environmental changes. As such, it helps farmers take necessary actions in advance of worse conditions. For valuable and unique aquarium fish, modified computer vision programs allow it to track individual fish and monitor its feeding activity to reflect the health status.

  • Internet of Things (IoT) - Raspberry Pi has a built-in Ethernet port and also can connect to the Internet wirelessly through USB WiFi dongle (or built-in WiFi in model 3). This makes the FeederBot an IoT device and enable other functionalities, such as wirelessly real-time control and monitor.

About

Future Direction

Fish FeederBot is an ongoing project. While the concept of monitoring fish feeding activity has been successfully implemented, there are still lots of work to do in order to achieve A.I. (Artificial Intelligence) fish health check. To obtain more precise fish count, other computer vision algorithms, such as Haar-cascade, will be used to recognize individual fish and distinguish different species. Also, machine learning algorithms will be used to train the computer to discern abnormal feeding patterns and then send alerts to fish keepers.

Designer

Kuan-Yu (Alex) Chen has a Ph.D. degree in Evolution, Ecology, and Organismal Biology, with a research focus on fish biology. He is also interested in aquarium and aquaculture automation. Since 2013, he has begun using two popular open source hardware platforms, Raspberry Pi and Arduino, to design gadgets for his aquariums. He hopes to bridge the gap between A.I. and fish culture or research.
