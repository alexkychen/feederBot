Concept

Fish feeding activity is an important indicator of fish heath status, but it can be difficult to determine if the feeding activity deviates from normal patterns. Toward this end, I have built a computerized device that can automatically feed and count fish. Fish feeding patterns, whether normal or abnormal, can be determined by time-series analyses and machine learning algorithms.

Fish feeding model: Fish feeding activity is quantified by counting the number of fish appeared within a feeding zone through time.

Feeding patterns deviates from normal. Slow feeding (red) indicates fish's appetite has decreased, whereas decreasing number of feeding fish (orange) implicates some fish did not come to eat.

Single Board Computer

Fish FeederBot is built based on Raspberry Pi - an open source, inexpensive, and expandable single board computer.

Camera Module

I use Raspberry Pi Camera module as the computer's eyes to monitor fish feeding activity. With relevant Python libraries, computer vision programming is used to count fish under the camera view.

Feeder Module

Feeding is controlled through a stepper motor so that the quantity of fish food can be precisely controlled and is adjustable.

Relay Module

To ensure the feeding activity concentrates under the camera view, a relay module is used to temporarily turn off the power of filters or pumps that produce water flow.

3D Printed Housing

Electronic components are protected by 3D printed plastic cases. The 3D printed parts can be customized; different materials and multiple colors are available.
